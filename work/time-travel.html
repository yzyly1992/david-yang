Time Traveler VR


David Yang / 2019 May

Virtual Reality Unity Landscape Architecture

The landscape process exerts a slow, silent, yet powerful impact on our world and daily lives. As contemporary humans, immersed in the struggles of the present, it becomes incredibly challenging for us to grasp the significance of past events or foresee the implications for the future. Here, the role of a landscape architect becomes crucial, as they serve as communicators and advocates, drawing attention to these issues for the public's awareness.


In this context, virtual reality emerges as a groundbreaking design medium, offering unique qualities that have the potential to effectively convey such complex problems compared to traditional media. This project aims to explore the possibilities of interpreting the landscape process through innovative means, leveraging immersive virtual environments and first-person storytelling.


By harnessing the power of virtual reality, the project seeks to create a profound and engaging experience for users, enabling them to comprehend the profound impacts of landscape processes in a more tangible and immersive manner. Through this novel approach, the project aspires to raise awareness, foster a deeper understanding of our environment, and encourage positive actions toward sustainable and responsible site development.









*Note: This is a Landscape Architecture Thesis Project instructed by Prof. Robyn Reed.
